EXAMPLES

I am Physics PhD student working in Dr. Rundle's group, developing and analyzing simulations of complex physical systems. I am a developer for Virtual California, a physics-based earthquake simulation code originally developed by my advisor Prof. John Rundle, with more recent contributions from Michael Sachs and Eric Heien. I continue to add new functionality to the simulator; e.g. computing co-seismic gravity changes. I am also developing a Python module for simulation data analysis and visualizations. Our goal is to provide an independent, physics-based method for computing earthquake forecasts, in addition to providing the computational infrastructure for generating ensembles of geodetic signals; e.g. surface displacements, gravity changes, sea level changes.
